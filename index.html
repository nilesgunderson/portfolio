About Me

Niles Gunderson, cello, performs extensively as a recitalist, orchestral, and chamber musician throughout North America, Europe, China, and Japan. His playing has been praised for its "elegance and ferocity" and he has appeared in venues including Carnegie Hall, David Geffen Hall, Alice Tully Hall, the Kaufman Center, the United Nations, Bowdoin International Music Festival, Texas Music Festival, and Tanglewood. He was worked with conductors including Kurt Masur, Valery Gergiev, Dale Clevenger, Horst Förster, Gerard Schwartz, among many others, He has been coached by the American String Quartet, Richard Danielpour, and the T’Ang Quartet. Additionally, he has premiered several new works with the contemporary ensemble mise-en including works by MiMirela Ivičevića amd Matthias Kranebitter. He performed the New York premiere of “Ronda” by Franco Donatoni at the Italian Academy at Columbia University as well as the New York premiere of Violin Concerto “Al-Andalus” by Mohammad Fairouz with the Mimesis Ensemble. He has performed Off-Broadway, and at the New York Fringe Festival. He can be heard on the soundtrack of several motion pictures including Salt, Sex and the City, True Grit, and appears regularly with major popular artists. He earned his Bachelor of Music from the Manhattan School of Music and continued graduate studies at La Scuola di Musica di Fiesole in Florence, Italy. His major teachers include Alan Stepansky, Marianne Chen, and Raymond Davis. He performs on a modern instrument by Jason Viseltear, New York City.
